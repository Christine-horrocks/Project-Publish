Sunday Dinner2 hrs

Shopping List

Roast Chicken

  • 1 chicken, about 1 1/2kg
  • 1 lemon halved
  • 1 onion, rounghly chopped
  • 2 carrots, roughly chopped
  • small bunch of freash thyme
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • salt and pepper

Gravy

  • 1 tbsp plain flour
  • 300ml chicken stock

Roast Potatoes

  • 1kg Maris Piper potatoes
  • 500ml water
  • 100ml olive oil
  • 2 tsp plain flour

Steamed Vegetables

  • 3 medium carrots
  • 1 medium head of broccoli

Yorkshire Puddings

  • 140g plain flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 175ml semi-skimmed milk

Equipment

2 roasting tins, 1 Yorkshire pudding tin, 1 large saucepan, 1 small saucepan, 1 sharp knife, 1 cutting board, 1 measuring jug, a set of scales, 1 whisk, 1 mixing bowl

Flow-Chart

Before you start make sure you have done the following:

  • Clear and wipedown all the worksurfaces
  • Collected together all your ingredients
  • Found all the neccessary equipment
  • Turned the oven on to 170 degrees

When ready press 'start' on the timer

At 0 mins

Place the roughly chopped onion and 2 roughly chopped carrots in the bottom of the roasting tin. Place the chicken on top of the vegetables. Put the two lemon halves inside the chicken cavity. Sprinkle the chicken with the tyhme and season generously with salt and pepper. Lightly drizzle the oil over the chicken and vegetables. Then place the chicken in the oven on the middle shelf.

At 45 mins

Peel the potatoes for the roast potatoes. Cut them roughly into 4 cm pieces. Place the potatoes in the large saucepan. Pour in enough water to cover the potatoes. Place the pan on a medium-high heat and leave to come to the boil. Then let them boil for two mins. While the water boils, put the olive oil into a roasting tin, and put the roasting tin on a lower shelf of the oven.

At 1 hr 10 mins

When the potatoes have boiled for 2 mins, drain the water throughly and shake the poatoes in the pan. This will give them fluffy edges to add crispyness. Pour the potatoes into the roating tin with the heated oil. Take care the oil may spit. Put the potatoes in the oven.

At 1 hr and 20 mins

Yorkshire Puddings: If you have not made Yorkshire pudding before, please watch this short video

Put the plain flour and salt into a mixing bowl. Add the eggs and beat slowly to a paste with a whisk. While still whisking pour in the milk, and contiue to whisk untill the mixture is the consistancy of single cream. Put the mixture to one side to rest.

At 1 hr and 30 mins

Pour 1 tbsp of oil into each cup of the Yorkshire pudding tin. Open the oven. Turn the roast potatoes. Also, spoon the juices from the bottom of the chicken roasting tin back over the top of the chicken. Place the Yorkshire pudding tin on to the lower shelf.

At 1hr and 35 mins

Wash the carrots and the broccoli. Slice the carrots and chop the broccoil.

At 1hr and 40 mins

  1. Remove the chicken from the overn, cover it with tinfoil and set aside to rest.
  2. Turn the roast potatoes.
  3. Take out the Yorkshire pudding tin out of the oven. Make sure it stays hot! Pour the Yorkshire pudding mix into the cups in the tin. Fill each cup halfway and return to the oven.

At 1 hr and 45 mins

To make the gravy, remove the chicken from the roasting tin and place on a plate. Carefull pour the fat out of the roasting tin, but leave behind the meat juice. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and meat juices left in the bottom of the tin. Mix thoroughly over a low heat. As you do this mix boiling water and a chicken stock cube. Pour the stock into the tin. Continue to mix and then pour into a small saucepan. Place this on a low heat, sturring occationally.

At 1hr and 50 mins

Wash the saucepan used for the potatoes and pour in 4 cm of boiling water. Add in a little salt. Add the carrots and broccoli, and steam for 10 mins.

At 2 hrs

  1. Remove the potatoes and the Yorkshire puddings from the oven.
  2. Drain the carrots and broccoli.
  3. Pour the gravy through a sive into a serving jug.
  4. Carve the chicken.

You are now ready to serve
